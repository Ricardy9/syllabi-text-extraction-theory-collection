          This course delves into the complex and multifaceted topic of violence, examining its causes,
          organization, and consequences in society. Students will investigate key questions such as:
          What triggers violence? Who participates in it, and why? How are violent actions and
          movements structured and organized? What relationships develop between violent groups and
          civilian populations? And how do governments and institutions respond to, counter, or
          perpetuate violence? The course covers a broad spectrum of violence, including but not limited
          to civil wars, violent crime, and police repression. These forms of violence will be analysed from
          multiple perspectives, offering students a comprehensive understanding of their origins,
          dynamics, and impacts. In addition to theoretical frameworks, students will engage with
          empirical data and real-world examples to understand how violence manifests in different
          contexts and the challenges it poses to societies.                      
                                                                                  
          Course Requirements:                                                    
                                                                                  
          Preparation and Participation (20%): Students are expected to complete all assigned
                                                                                  
          readings before class and come prepared to actively engage in discussions.
                                                                                  
          Reading Responses (20%) (1000 words): Students may enrol in the course for 3, 4, or 5
          units, which determines their workload for reading responses: 3 units (one reading response); 4
          units (two reading responses); 5 units (three reading responses). To be uploaded to canvas one
          day before the corresponding class discussion. Students writing on that week are expected to
          help lead our class discussion.                                         
                                                                                  
          Peer Engagement: All students are required to read and review the reading responses of
          their peers before the seminar meets.                                   
                                                                                  
          Case Study Presentation (20%): Each student will join a group of 2-3 students to present a case
          study on a country-specific conflict. Presentations will take place during two different days in
          class days (Nov 11) and (Dec 4). A sign-up sheet will be provided for students to choose their
          topics.                                                                 
                                                                                  
          Movies and movie review (10%): Students will be watching various movies during class on civil
          wars and conflicts and deliver a one-page critical summary of the movie.
                                                                                  
          Final Examination (30%): A take-home exam will be distributed during Week 10, with
          submissions due during the final exam week. The number of questions students must answer
          corresponds to their enrolled units: 1 question (3 units), 2 questions (4 units) and 3 questions
          (5 units).
