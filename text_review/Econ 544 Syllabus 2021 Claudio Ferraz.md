          Course Objective:                                                       
          The previous course on the sequence of Political Economy focused on long-run persistence,
          autocracies and civil conflict. This course will discuss the reasons why countries democratize,
                                                                                  
          what makes people vote in democracies and what type of policies voters reward. We will
          introduce and test basic models of political competition and present failures of democracies
          that occur in developing countries such as clientelism, vote buying and political violence. We
          will then introduce agency models and discuss problems of asymmetric information that leads
          to corruption and discuss anti-corruption policies. We will then move to lectures on financing
          democracies with campaign contributions and the role of lobbying for policy formation. We
          will discuss the role of media in democracies and finish the course with lectures on internet
          and social media and the rise of extremism and populism around the world.
